Visual Aesthetic Direction

What should the 3D world feel like? Pick the mood that fits your identity as a systems/security engineer.

A. Dark Void + Neon Wireframe

Deep black space. Nodes are glowing wireframe geometries (icospheres, octahedra). Edges are thin neon lines with particle flow. Minimal, elegant, hacker aesthetic. Think: network monitoring dashboard in a sci-fi film.

Pros

  • Strong systems engineer identity
  • Performant (fewer polygons)
  • High contrast, readable overlays

Cons

  • Can feel cold/generic if not careful

B. Volumetric Depth + Solid Geometry

Deep blue-purple atmosphere with fog and bloom. Nodes are solid 3D shapes with subtle materials. Edges are volumetric beams. More cinematic, more atmosphere. Think: an observatory or control room floating in space.

Pros

  • More depth and atmosphere
  • Feels premium / cinematic
  • Closer to activetheory.net feel

Cons

  • Heavier on GPU (fog, bloom, post-processing)
  • Harder to get right

C. Monochrome Particle Cloud

Near-black void. Nodes are clusters of white particles that swarm and breathe. Edges are faint particle streams. Almost no color — identity comes from motion and density. Think: data visualized as star systems.

Pros

  • Extremely elegant and distinctive
  • Motion carries the experience
  • Unique — doesn't look like typical 3D portfolio

Cons

  • Risky — needs great motion design to not feel empty
  • Less immediately "impressive" in screenshots
